Support the Fight to End Poaching

In the past decade, the elephant population has dropped by 62% and at this rate, they could become mostly extinct by the end of the next decade. Elephants poaching is driven by the illegal ivory trade to asian markets, which has become more and more profitable with the decline of the elephant population.

Currently, the world is losing more elephants than the population can reproduce. Elephants are a keystone species and the loss of elephants would cause immense damage to the ecosystems in which they live.

In the last century, the rhino population fell from 500,000 to 29,000 today. Similar to the elephants' ivory tusks, rhinos are being poached for their valuable horns, which are traded illegally to asian markets where it is used for traditional medicine or art as a status symbol for the rich.

Due to conservationists' efforts, the population of some rhino species have been slowly increasing or stable; however, 3 species still remain on the critically endangered list, with 2 of those species having no more than 100 individuals left.
